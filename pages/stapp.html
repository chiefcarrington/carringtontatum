It's hard to find good help during breaks

April 3, 2019

Read the story

Only a few days before the end of winter break, San Marcos police killed a man and offered few details prior to a press conference. As editor-in-chief of the University Star, I was in town early to get my ducks in a row before the start of a new long semester. Typically an event like this would fall to my news department with an editor and several reporters. However, the plight of a student newsroom is keeping up with coverage during the academic breaks.

When people ask me what I do as editor-in-chief, I never know what to tell them except, "everything everyone else doesn't do". This is one of those moments when my news staff is trying to squeeze the last out of winter break, and the managing editor is out of town. While shot calling from the newsroom is more my job description, I had no choice but to gear up and cover it myself.

I never hate opportunities to cover news however, because my background in opinions means I never got the opportunity to be a regular reporter at The Star. Keeping the digital product in mind, I attended the press conference to run photo and facebook live, both of which are embedded in the brief I wrote.

I've always encouraged my reporters to be multimedia journalists. Never approach a story just to write, but rather to consider all of the mediums that could be applied to a story and choose which would be best for the issue. In this case, we had no details about the officer-involved shooting. That is why I decided to live stream because events like these are liable to have a lot of social and ethical conflict attached and the community was just as curious as we were to learn the details.

While simple, covering this happening was the pure fun in reporting that I often miss while being an editor. But I think it also highlights the sacrifices you make as a student journalist. We aren't always regular students who go to class, go home, and hang out on the weekends. We often make sacrifices of our personal time that aren't congruent with our pay, only because we love the work we do and the community we serve.
