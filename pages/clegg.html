Capturing campus climate

April 3, 2019

Read the story

We had no idea Conner Clegg would be the Student Government president to make state and national news. It's almost blurry now to differentiate the number of hearings and trials we covered as students appealed Clegg's verdict time and time again.

I was the opinions editor at the time of this hearing. The impeachment of a Student Government president for a myriad of offenses leaves plenty of opinions to be written. However, at the moment I felt that this was something to be well documented visually because it was the first Student Government president in Texas State history to be deposed.

Naturally, we had no idea how much these photos would capture the climate of campus at the time. Although it's unspoken, I think Conner Clegg represented much more than just the local grief of the student body. I think students saw him as a much more accessible extension of the prejudice displayed by Donald Trump.

Anyone who has interacted with Clegg knows his persona is a well-practiced imitation of Donald Trump. In a time when Texas State was a battleground of idealists from Nazi flyers, to racially charged opinions columns, and Trump's election, he was a local manifestation of a nationally shared frustration.
