About Carrington

Exploring unfamiliar spaces and returning has a guide has been a defining motif of my life.

The products of west Texas and south Dallas, my parents worked to make me the first of the family to access the promises of suburban life in Garland. Navigating suburbia and its brand of racism equipped me to be the first in the family to earn a degree from Texas State University. While in San Marcos, I was the first Black editor in chief of the University Star.

I took these experiences into journalism at the Texas Tribune and the Dallas Morning News because I saw how information can empower. Regular working people need a news reporter to ask questions on their behalf. I’m on a mission to fill news desserts for Black communities niche and wide.

Let me know what questions I can ask for you.
